# STRAT 412 – Passion Project Guidelines
**Winter 2026 | Sections 1–2**

---

## Objective

Practice using one or more course software platforms (**Python, SQL, Tableau**) applied to a personal passion or interest — a business, side hustle, internship, or hobby (e.g., NBA, college basketball, skiing).

---

## Individual vs. Group Structure

- Groups of **2–4 students** are allowed.
- **Minimum platforms used = number of group members** (e.g., 2 members → 2 platforms).
- R from STRAT 402 counts as one platform.
- Groups may include students from either section.

---

## Problem Selection

Identify a **business problem or set of strategic questions** addressable with at least one course platform:

| Platform | Best for |
|----------|----------|
| **Tableau** | Dashboards and data visualization |
| **Python** | Web scraping, data extraction, automation |
| **SQL** | Generating insights from large databases |

The problem can be **real** or **fictitious but realistic**. If fictitious, you must justify why it is a likely problem for your focal company/organization.

---

## Project Deliverables

1. **Slide deck** presented to the class
2. **Data and code** used to produce analysis and results
3. **Link to a recorded presentation** (≤ 6 minutes)

All files must be organized and annotated so that anyone can follow your analysis from raw data to final recommendations.

## Checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1 – Brainstorming Ideas
Submit a list of **10 different businesses, organizations, industries, or interests** that could serve as a basis for your project.

### Checkpoint 2 – Getting Feedback
Post your **top 3 favorite/most feasible ideas** on Digital Dialog. Observe peers' ideas, give feedback, and consider collaboration if interests align.

### Checkpoint 3 – Making the Choice
After receiving feedback from Prof. Lewis (office hours, Slack, or email), choose **one idea** from Checkpoint 2. Submit a **one-page proposal** covering:
- The question/problem
- The type of analysis
- The data you will need

### Checkpoint 4 – Outlining Your Presentation
Submit a **mock-up slide deck outline** (not the finished product) to demonstrate progress and receive feedback.

## Evaluation Rubric

### Questions Considered

**Structure**
- Is the report/presentation complete and cohesive?
- Is the situation clearly communicated?
- Is the complication clearly communicated?
- Is the question/problem statement clear?
- Is the solution consistent with the complication and problem statement?

**Quality of Analysis**
- Is the analysis convincing? Would a manager rely on it?
- Are data visualizations well-constructed?
- Are limitations adequately addressed?
- Can someone follow the analysis from raw data to final numbers?

**Assessment of Effort**
- Is the slide deck professional?
- Is the presentation well-rehearsed and adequately prepared?
- Does the individual/group demonstrate serious effort and creativity?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Absolutely Yes |
| 4 | Yes |
| 3 | Sort of |
| 2 | No |
| 1 | Absolutely No |
